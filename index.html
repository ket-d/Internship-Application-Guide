Why Learn From Me?

Hello! I'm Ketura Yaje Gwei.


I am a biochemist from Cameroon who is passionate about research, structural biology, and helping students access international opportunities.


Coming from a resource-limited background, I understand how confusing it can be to search for internships abroad, contact professors, prepare application documents, and compete with applicants from around the world. Through determination and continuous learning, I successfully secured competitive international research internships at some of the world's leading institutions, including:


  • 🇨🇭 EPFL (École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne), Switzerland
  • 🇩🇪 Max Planck Institute for Infection Biology, Germany
  • 🇩🇪 Admission into the prestigious Matter to Life Programme in Germany.

Along the way, I learned how to:


  • Find hidden internship opportunities.
  • Write competitive CVs and resumes.
  • Write strong motivation letters.
  • Email professors professionally.
  • Prepare successful scholarship applications.
  • Prepare for interviews.
  • Navigate visa and relocation processes.

This website is my way of giving back. Instead of spending months figuring everything out alone like I did, you can learn from my experiences, avoid common mistakes, and become a more competitive applicant for international internships and research programs.

Types of International Internships

Research Internships

Research opportunities at universities and institutes for students in biology, medicine, chemistry, engineering, computer science and related fields.

Summer Research Programs

Fully-funded or partially funded programs lasting between 6 and 12 weeks, typically offered during university holidays.

Industry Internships

Gain practical experience in biotechnology, pharmaceuticals, engineering, technology and other industries.

NGO & Global Health Internships

Work with international organizations addressing public health, sustainability, education and community development.

Application Tips

Build a Strong CV

Highlight your achievements, projects, leadership, volunteering, technical skills and research experience.

Write a Great Motivation Letter

Explain your motivation, research interests and why you are a good fit for the opportunity.

Email Professors Professionally

Learn how to write concise and effective emails that increase your chances of receiving a response.

Apply Widely

Do not rely on a single application. Competitive applicants submit many high-quality applications.
